Your Travel Guide

Agroturizëm Gjepali is a countryside venue near Gjepalaj and Shijak, close to Durrës and within easy reach of Tirana Airport. Here is everything you need to plan your journey.

By Air & Road

The wedding venue is Agroturizëm Gjepali in the Gjepalaj/Shijak area. The simplest arrival point is Tirana International Airport, followed by a pre-booked transfer, taxi, or rental car to the venue or your accommodation.

From Tirana Airport to the Venue Area

Tirana Airport is the most practical gateway. The final stretch to Agroturizëm Gjepali is by road, so plan this part before you arrive.

  • Pre-book your transfer if possible

    A private transfer or taxi is the easiest option, especially if you are arriving with luggage or late in the day. Ask your accommodation to help arrange pickup if you are staying in Durrës, Shijak, or Tirana.

  • Use Durrës as the easiest coastal base

    Durrës is much closer to the venue than Albania's southern coast and has a wide range of hotels and apartments. It is a practical choice if you want to stay near the sea while keeping the wedding transfer manageable.

  • Consider Tirana for city convenience

    Tirana has the widest hotel choice, restaurants, and nightlife. It is a good option if you want a city break, but allow extra time on the wedding day because traffic can vary.

  • Rental car only if you are comfortable driving

    A car gives flexibility for beaches and day trips, but it is not necessary for the wedding itself if you use the coordinated transport. Please plan a safe return if you will be drinking.

Timing tip: If you arrive on Friday 4th September, try to land with enough daylight to reach your accommodation calmly, check in, and arrange your wedding-day pickup.

Around Gjepali

Getting Around

Wedding Transport

We are coordinating guest transport for the wedding day. Pickup points and exact times will be shared closer to the date once we know where guests are staying. Please confirm your transport needs in the RSVP.

Taxis & Drivers

Taxis and private drivers are the most practical way to move between the venue, Durrës, Shijak, Tirana, and the airport. Agree the price before departure or ask your hotel to arrange a trusted driver.

Walking

The venue is in a countryside setting, so it is not a place to rely on walking between hotels and the celebration. Choose walking only for exploring Durrës seafront, Tirana neighbourhoods, or your accommodation area.

Ride Apps

Uber and Bolt are not reliable assumptions in Albania. Hotel-arranged taxis, WhatsApp contact with a driver, or a pre-booked transfer are safer choices for airport and late-night journeys.

Recommended Arrival

We suggest arriving on Thursday 3rd or Friday 4th September to settle in and make your transfer plans comfortably. Departing on Sunday 6th gives you a relaxed morning after the celebration.

Good to Know

Practical Information

Currency & Payment

Albanian Lek (ALL). Euros are accepted in many tourist-facing places, but Lek is preferred and usually simpler. Cards are common in hotels and larger restaurants; keep some cash for taxis and smaller places.

Language

Albanian (Shqip). English is common in many hotels and tourist-facing businesses in Tirana and Durrës. A few words of Albanian are always appreciated.

Mobile & Internet

Check whether your EU roaming package includes Albania. Local SIM cards and eSIMs are easy options if your plan does not include affordable roaming.

Safety

The area is generally friendly for visitors. Use normal travel awareness, pre-arrange late-night transport, and do not rely on walking back from the countryside venue.

Weather in September

Expect warm late-summer weather. The venue is outdoors, so bring breathable clothing for the day and a light layer for the evening.

Electricity

European Type C/F sockets are used. EU devices work without an adaptor; UK and US visitors should bring one.
