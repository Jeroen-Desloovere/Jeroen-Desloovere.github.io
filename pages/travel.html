Albania Notes

Travel

A warm and practical guide for getting to Albania, choosing where to stay, reaching Agroturizëm Gjepali, and making a little more of the trip if you would like to.

Quick Overview

The wedding venue is Agroturizëm Gjepali, located in the village of Gjepalaj, near Shijak, between Tirana and Durrës. The nearest airport is Tirana International Airport. This page keeps everything in one place: flights, transport, where to base yourself, and ideas for nearby exploring.

Getting to Albania

Arriving by Air

Tirana International Airport is the nearest airport. The venue is about 30 minutes from the airport without traffic, so allow a bit longer with traffic.

Airport

You will probably fly into Tirana International Airport. Most likely, you will first go to your hotel rather than travelling directly from or to the airport.

Short Stay

If you are only coming for the wedding, flying in the day before and flying out the day after, you do not necessarily need to rent a car.

Longer Stay

You can rent a car if you want, especially if you are staying longer in Albania.

Passport tip: If you are travelling from the EU, Canada, or the US, bring your passport so you can use the electronic gates at the airport. Please also make sure your passport is valid for travel.

Where Should You Stay?

Choosing Your Base

There is no single right answer; it depends on whether you want the shortest journey, the beach, the city, or a quieter holiday feeling.

Near Agroturizëm Gjepali/Shijak area

Best if you want the shortest and easiest journey after the wedding.

The venue is located in the village of Gjepalaj, near Shijak, between Tirana and Durrës. Staying nearby is the most convenient option for the wedding itself. The area is peaceful and rural, though there is little in the immediate surroundings for sightseeing or exploring.

Durrës & the Seafront

Best if you want beaches, restaurants, and a relaxed coastal atmosphere.

Durrës offers a lively seafront promenade, plenty of restaurants and cafés, and long sandy beaches that are ideal for families. It is also conveniently located between the wedding venue and the airport, making it a practical base for the weekend.

Tirana

Best if you enjoy city life and want the greatest choice of restaurants, cafés, and nightlife.

Albania's capital is the most convenient option if you are arriving late, staying a few extra days, or looking to explore beyond the wedding celebrations. Tirana has a vibrant food scene, lively neighbourhoods, and easy access to the airport.

Lalëz Bay & Cape of Rodon

Best if you are looking for a beach holiday atmosphere.

This area offers a quieter, more resort-style experience than Durrës, with beaches, seaside resorts, and a more relaxed holiday feel. Having a car is helpful for exploring the surrounding area and reaching the wedding venue.

What Can I Do Nearby?

Easy Half-Day Trips

Durrës Old Town & Seafront

Enjoy a stroll along the waterfront, stop for fresh seafood, and explore some of the city's highlights, including the Roman Amphitheatre and Venetian Tower. The recently reopened National Archaeological Museum is also worth a visit.

Tirana

Perfect for an afternoon or evening of cafés, bars, restaurants, and people-watching. If you have a full day, consider taking the cable car up Mount Dajti for panoramic views and a break from the city.

Beach Day: Lalëz Bay, Cape of Rodon or Golem

Ideal if you simply want to relax by the sea without travelling far from the wedding area.

What If You Stay a Couple of Days More?

Easy Day Trips

Krujë

A beautiful hilltop town with a traditional bazaar, mountain views, and a strong connection to Albania's national hero, Skanderbeg. It is a lovely introduction to Albanian culture and easy to combine with a relaxed wander through the old streets.

Berat

Often called the "City of a Thousand Windows," Berat is one of Albania's most beautiful destinations. Its white Ottoman houses, castle area, riverside setting, and UNESCO-listed centre make it an excellent day trip or overnight stay.

Tirana's Bunk'Art Museums

A memorable stop while exploring Tirana, especially if you want to understand more of Albania's recent past. The museums are atmospheric, easy to visit, and pair well with an afternoon of cafés, restaurants, and city wandering.

Apollonia

A peaceful day trip through rolling countryside, with ancient ruins, temples, city walls, and a beautifully preserved monastery. Apollonia is one of Albania's most important archaeological sites.

What If I Have a Bit More Time?

Longer Albania Ideas

Shkodër & the Albanian Alps

If you are interested in nature and outdoor adventures, northern Albania is well worth exploring. Shkodër is a charming city on the shores of Lake Shkodër and serves as the gateway to the Albanian Alps. From here you can start your journey to the mountain landscapes, including the villages of Theth and Valbonë, which are known for their dramatic scenery and excellent hiking trails.

The Albanian Riviera

Home to some of Albania's best beaches and coastal scenery. While too far for a wedding-weekend excursion, it is well worth considering if you are planning a longer stay. For beach lovers we would recommend renting a car and exploring the beaches of Livadh, Filikuri, Gjipe, Borsh, Ksamil and other spots in between.

Gjirokastër

A UNESCO-listed stone city with beautifully preserved Ottoman architecture, historic streets, and mountain views. Best visited as part of a longer journey through southern Albania.

How to Get Around

Cars, Taxis & Local Transport

Renting a Car

The easiest and smoothest way to get around Albania is by renting your own car and driving around. The style of driving in Albania can be a little more assertive than in Northern Europe, so please keep that in mind and allow yourself extra time to travel. Most of the main roads in Albania are in good shape but mountain roads and roads to smaller villages might be narrow, winding, or less well maintained.

Buses & Furgons

Albania has an extensive and affordable network of buses and furgons (shared minibuses) connecting most cities, towns, and many villages. They are a popular way to travel locally, although schedules can be less predictable than in Northern Europe and information is not always available online. If you are comfortable with a bit of flexibility, they can be a convenient and authentic way to explore the country without renting a car. The buses and furgons leave mostly from central areas. If you are in doubt, ask the people around you; they will be more than happy to help.

Taxis

Taxis are widely available in Albanian cities and are generally affordable compared to most of Western and Northern Europe. Ride services such as Uber are not currently available, but local taxi apps like Speed Taxi and Patoko in Tirana offer a convenient way to book rides with transparent pricing. Some companies, such as Green Taxi (+355 69 800 2000), operate 24 hours a day and can be booked simply by phone, with English-speaking operators available. Hotels can also arrange a licensed taxi for you.

Practical

Some Extra Tips

  • Can You Use Your Phone Data? Albania is not part of the EU roaming zone, so using your normal mobile plan can be expensive. We recommend checking with your provider before travelling. Usually, the easiest and cheapest option is to buy a local SIM card or eSIM. These are easy to set up and offer generous data packages for a good price. The main mobile providers in Albania are One Albania and Vodafone Albania, both of which offer SIM cards and eSIMs that can be purchased before arrival or at the airport. Mobile coverage is generally excellent in cities, along the coast, and in most tourist areas, but can be weaker in remote mountain areas.
